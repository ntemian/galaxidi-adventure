# Το Μυστήριο του Γαλαξειδίου — Story Bible

## Logline

A father and his two children arrive in Galaxidi to visit their great-grandfather's house — and discover a 120-year-old treasure hunt left by Captain Visvikis, the last captain who believed Galaxidi could be great again.

## Theme

**"Κράτα την αλληλεγγύη. Άλλαξε το πλοίο."** — Two lessons woven into one story: (1) The power of solidarity — the captains who protected each other through αλληλασφάλεια. (2) The danger of not adapting — the captains who mastered sail but refused steam. The treasure isn't gold — it's understanding that you must hold onto what binds you together while having the courage to change.

## Setting

**Galaxidi (Γαλαξίδι)**, Corinthian Gulf, Greece. Late September 2026. End of summer — golden light, warm stone, the sea impossibly blue. A town that was once the greatest shipbuilding port in Greece (300 ships, 6,000 residents at its peak in 1870), now a quiet fishing village that holds its secrets close.

## Characters

### Ntemis (Ντέμης) — The Father
- Calm, warm, thoughtful. Sees beauty in small things.
- Great-grandson of Captain Loukas Visvikis. Knows Galaxidi from childhood summers.
- Returns to settle the grandfather's house (inheritance). Doesn't expect an adventure.

### Ajax (Αίαντας) — The Son
- Energetic, curious, brave. Always wants to explore.
- Asks questions about everything. First to volunteer for adventure.
- "Μπαμπά, πάμε!" is his catchphrase.
- Can reach small spaces, digs when needed (gameplay mechanic).

### Clio (Κλειώ) — The Daughter
- Observant, creative, wise beyond her years. Named after the Muse of History.
- Notices details others miss — looks where no one else does. The emotional heart of the story.
- Draws people and moments in her notebook.
- "Κοιτάξτε..." is her catchphrase — she's always pointing out what matters.
- A cat follows her throughout the entire game.

### NPCs

| NPC | Scene | Role |
|-----|-------|------|
| **Σταύρος / Stavros** | Port | Old fisherman, mends nets by the quay. His grandfather knew Visvikis. Points family to the cave |
| **Επιμελητής / Curator** | Museum | Thin man with reading glasses and passion. Teaches the golden age, the 300 ships, and the fatal refusal of steam |
| **Άθος / Athos** | Liotrivi | Lawyer and owner. Knows every old family's history. Read Visvikis's will — "left everything to Galaxidi" |
| **Άκης / Akis** | Waterfront | Flute player (φλογέρα), friend of Ntemis. Knows Galaxidi history. Points to museum |
| **Στάθης / Stathis** | Waterfront | Guitar player, friend of Ntemis. Points to Liotrivi (Athos). Plays the old sailors' melody |
| **Γιάννης / Giannis** | Windmill | Owner of the old windmill above town. Quiet, sees everything from above. Gives family the lantern |
| **Παπάς / Papas** | Church | Warm, slightly mischievous. Visvikis was his church's greatest benefactor |
| **Χρυσόστομος / Chrysostomos** | Boat | Old sailor, boats the family to Agios Georgios. Knew Ntemis's father, waited a lifetime for this day |
| **Ghost of Captain Visvikis** | Graveyard | Silent, weathered, proud. Waited 120 years. Points to the church. Finds peace when the stone is placed |

## The Quest

Captain Loukas Visvikis (1841-1903), the last great captain of Galaxidi, traveled to the East and returned with a jade stone and a fortune. He hid his treasure for someone who would love Galaxidi enough to find it. He left a letter in his house with instructions. The family must follow the trail: house → port → waterfront (friends) → museum → liotrivi → windmill → cave → graveyard → church → boat → island → treasure.

### Key Items
- **Visvikis's Letter** (Scene 2) — The quest hook. "Find the green stone."
- **Captain's Photograph** (Scene 2) — From the house, shows Visvikis in captain's dress.
- **The Green Stone / Jade** (Scene 8) — Found in Karkaros cave where "the earth speaks." Warm to the touch.
- **Brass Lantern / Φανάρι** (Scene 7) — Given by Giannis at the windmill. Used in the cave.
- **Nautical Chart** (Scene 10) — Hidden under a floor tile in Agios Nikolaos church. Marks Agios Georgios island.
- **The Treasure Chest** (Scene 12) — On Agios Georgios island. Contains gold coins, jade necklace, the original αλληλασφάλεια ledger, and Visvikis's final letter.

## Scene Structure (12 Scenes + Epilogue)

### SCENE 1 — ΑΦΙΞΗ (Arrival)

*The family arrives at Galaxidi by car. Late afternoon, golden hour.*

**Story beats**:
1. Family walks through the crescent harbor. A fisherman nods. A cat follows Clio.
2. Ntemis: "Φτάσαμε. Πρέπει να βρούμε το σπίτι του παππού."
3. Ajax: "Ποιου παππού;" — Ntemis: "Του παππού μου. Κανένας δεν έχει μπει εδώ χρόνια. Κληρονομιά."
4. Clio: "Θα έχει φαντάσματα;" — Ntemis: "...Όχι, Κλειώ. Θα έχει σκόνη."
5. They find the house on a narrow lane. Old stone, blue door, bougainvillea. The key barely turns.
6. The door opens. Dust and memory pour out.

**Gameplay**: Tutorial — learn to click, walk, look. Explore harbor (bollards, boats, cat). Walk to house → exit to Scene 2.

**Music**: Warm acoustic guitar. The main theme, simple and solo. Cicadas. Mediterranean afternoon.

---

### SCENE 2 — ΤΟ ΣΠΙΤΙ ΤΟΥ ΠΑΠΠΟΥ (The Grandfather's House)

*Dark interior. Ntemis opens shutters one by one. Golden light floods in to reveal a captain's house frozen in 1903.*

**Story beats**:
1. Model ships on shelves, brass instruments, charts on walls. A faded photograph of Captain Visvikis.
2. Clio: "Ποιος είναι αυτός;" — Ntemis: "Ο Καπετάν Βισβίκης. Ο προπάππους μου."
3. Ajax finds a locked desk drawer. Clio finds the key in a copper pot on the mantle.
4. Inside the drawer: **Visvikis's letter** (1887). Sealed with wax. Instructions to find "the green stone" hidden "where the earth speaks — where rocks become cave and sound never leaves."
5. Ajax: "'Φέρε τη σε μένα'... Είναι νεκρός εδώ και εκατό χρόνια." Clio: "Στο νεκροταφείο, βλάκα." Ntemis: "...Κλειώ." Clio: "Τι; Λογικό είναι."

**Inventory**: + Γράμμα Βισβίκη, + Φωτογραφία Καπετάνιου

**Gameplay**: Open shutters (light reveals room). Examine photo, ships, instruments. Find locked drawer → key in pot → letter. Quest begins.

**Music**: Crossfade from acoustic guitar to mysterious piano. Swells when letter is read — adventure theme enters for the first time.

---

### SCENE 3 — ΤΟ ΛΙΜΑΝΙ (Asking Around)

*The harbor in early evening. Taverna lights coming on. Fishermen pulling nets.*

**Story beats**:
1. Show letter around town. Most shrug. One old woman crosses herself. A taverna owner laughs: "Βισβίκης! Ο τρελός καπετάνιος!"
2. **Stavros**, mending nets by the quay, goes still at the name. His grandfather was in Visvikis's crew.
3. Stavros: "Ταξίδεψε στη Σμύρνη, στην Αλεξάνδρεια, στη Βηρυτό. Λέγανε ότι γύρισε με κάτι... κάτι πράσινο."
4. On the clue "where the earth speaks": "Ο Κάρκαρος. Το σπήλαιο πίσω από την πλατεία Μάμα. Μπαίνεις μέσα και η φωνή σου γυρίζει πίσω τρεις φορές."
5. Warning: "Ο Βισβίκης δεν ήταν απλός καπετάνιος. Ήταν ο τελευταίος που πίστευε ότι το Γαλαξίδι μπορούσε να ξαναγίνει μεγάλο."
6. Clio asks the cat if it knows where the cave is. The cat walks toward the museum.

**Gameplay**: Show letter to 2-3 NPCs (comic responses). Find Stavros — main dialog tree. Stavros suggests museum for more. Exit to Museum.

**Music**: Bouzouki, lively harbor warmth. Shifts subtler when Stavros speaks about Visvikis.

---

### SCENE 4 — Η ΠΑΡΕΑ (The Friends)

*Waterfront promenade, golden hour. The anchor monument. Akis and Stathis sit on a blue bench, playing music — φλογέρα and guitar.*

**Story beats**:
1. Ntemis spots two old friends on the waterfront. Recognition. Warm reunion — hugs, laughter, years compressed into smiles.
2. Akis (φλογέρα) and Stathis (guitar) play a slow sailors' melody. The harbor listens.
3. Ntemis: "Πόσα χρόνια, ρε παιδιά;" Akis: "Αρκετά. Αλλά το Γαλαξίδι δε σ' αφήνει να ξεχάσεις."
4. Ajax and Clio listen. Clio asks about the melody — "Λυπημένο αλλά ωραίο." Stathis: "Ήταν το τραγούδι των ναυτικών. Το τραγουδούσαν όταν έφευγαν."
5. Akis knows Galaxidi history well. Points to the museum: "Αν ψάχνεις για τον Βισβίκη, πρέπει να δεις το μουσείο. Εκεί ζει η ιστορία."
6. Stathis points to Liotrivi: "Και μίλα στον Άθο. Ξέρει κάθε οικογένεια, κάθε μυστικό."
7. Clio draws the two musicians on the bench: "Σαν να παίζουν για τη θάλασσα."

**Gameplay**: Walk along waterfront. Approach bench → reunion cutscene. Talk to Akis (museum hint). Talk to Stathis (Liotrivi/Athos hint). Listen to music (sailors' melody — this melody returns in the epilogue). Clio notebook moment. Exit to Museum or Liotrivi.

**Music**: Live φλογέρα + guitar, a Mediterranean sailors' melody. Warm, nostalgic, bittersweet. This melody becomes a motif — it returns in the epilogue.

---

### SCENE 5 — ΤΟ ΝΑΥΤΙΚΟ ΜΟΥΣΕΙΟ (The Maritime Museum)

*Warm wood, naval flags, model ships in glass cases. Captain portraits — proud men with beards and dress coats.*

**Story beats**:
1. The Curator lights up at the name Visvikis. Leads them to his portrait — the same face as the house photograph, but younger.
2. The golden age: "Τριακόσια καράβια. Έξι χιλιάδες κάτοικοι. Το Γαλαξίδι ήταν η ναυτική πρωτεύουσα της Ελλάδας."
3. The steamship corner — a small, iron, ugly model next to the graceful schooners: "...ήρθε ο ατμός. Κι αυτοί που ήξεραν τα πάντα για τα πανιά, αρνήθηκαν να μάθουν κάτι καινούριο."
4. Ajax's key question: "Δηλαδή... ήταν οι καλύτεροι, κι αυτό τους έκανε χειρότερους;" Curator: "Ακριβώς, μικρέ μου."
5. Clio finds the αλληλασφάλεια display — a copy of the 1860 mutual insurance ledger. 104 captains signed.
6. Ntemis: "Μήπως στο Λιοτρίβι;" — someone there might know more.

**Gameplay**: Explore museum — model ships, portraits, instruments. Curator dialog tree. Steamship corner — pivotal narrative turn. Clio finds αλληλασφάλεια display. Exit to Liotrivi.

**Music**: Reflective piano and strings. Grand for golden age. Minor key at steamship corner — the sound of something ending.

---

### SCENE 6 — ΛΙΟΤΡΙΒΙ (The Old Olive Press)

*Evening. Stone walls, amber lanterns, the old olive press preserved as decor. Athos behind the bar, reading glasses perched on sharp features.*

**Story beats**:
1. Ntemis asks about Visvikis. Athos sets down his glass. His lawyer's eyes sharpen.
2. Athos: "Ξέρεις ότι διάβασα τη διαθήκη του; Επαγγελματική διαστροφή — ψάχνω παλιά έγγραφα. Τα άφησε όλα 'στο Γαλαξίδι.' Όχι σε πρόσωπο. Στην πόλη."
3. Ajax blurts: "Εμείς ξέρουμε! 'Εκεί που η γη μιλάει.'" Athos confirms: the Karkaros cave behind Plateia Mama.
4. He pours tsipouro for Ntemis, lemonade for the kids. Leans in: "Μη με ρωτήσεις αν αξίζεις. Πήγαινε και δείξ' το."
5. Clio draws Athos in her notebook: "Μοιάζεις με δικαστή." Athos: "Χειρότερα. Δικηγόρος."

**Gameplay**: Talk to Athos — confirms cave, reveals will details. Examine olive press, bottles, photos. Clio draws Athos (notebook moment). Exit to Windmill or Plateia Mama → Cave.

**Music**: Rebetiko warmth. Bouzouki and guitar, amber and smoky. Quieter when Athos speaks about Visvikis's will.

---

### SCENE 7 — Ο ΜΥΛΟΣ (The Windmill)

*Old stone windmill on the hill above town. Panoramic view of the gulf, the harbor, the mountains. Wildflowers between cracked stones. Wind hums through the ruins.*

**Story beats**:
1. The family climbs the path. Giannis stands at the windmill door, watching the gulf below.
2. Giannis: "Ξέρεις γιατί αγόρασα τον μύλο; Γιατί από εδώ βλέπεις τα πάντα."
3. He shows them the view — the harbor, the cave entrance below, the church, the islands. The whole quest laid out from above.
4. Giannis points out the cave entrance from this vantage point: "Εκεί κάτω. Πίσω από την πλατεία Μάμα. Θα χρειαστείτε φως."
5. He gives them a brass lantern — old, well-kept, nautical. "Ήταν του πατέρα μου. Ναυτικός. Πάρ' το."
6. Clio: "Σαν τον Βισβίκη. Αυτός ήθελε να μείνει κάτι." Giannis smiles but says nothing.
7. Ajax: "Βλέπω το νησάκι! Εκεί θα πάμε;" Ntemis: "Αν βρούμε αυτό που ψάχνουμε στο σπήλαιο."

**Inventory**: + Φανάρι (lantern)

**Gameplay**: Climb path to windmill. Talk to Giannis — panoramic view reveals quest geography. Receive lantern. Look through telescope (optional — see island detail). Exit down to Plateia Mama → Cave.

**Music**: Wind and open air. Sparse acoustic guitar, birdsong, distant town sounds below. Panoramic calm — the moment before descent.

---

### SCENE 8 — ΤΟ ΣΠΗΛΑΙΟ (The Cave of Karkaros)

*Plateia Mama. Behind old crates and overgrown bushes: the cave entrance. Inside: dripping water, echo, darkness.*

**Story beats**:
1. Ntemis uses phone flashlight. Walls covered in old marks — ship names, initials, dates scratched into stone over centuries.
2. Ajax: "Μπαμπά, κοίτα — ο Βισβίκης ήταν εδώ. 'Λ.Β. 1887.'"
3. They search. Ntemis at ground level. Ajax checks crevices at his height. Nothing.
4. Clio looks UP — the angle only a seven-year-old would try. She sees it. Wedged into a crack above eye level: **THE GREEN STONE.** Smooth, dark green jade, roughly fist-sized. Warm to the touch.
5. Ntemis: "Νεφρίτης. Jade. Από την Ανατολή, σίγουρα." Ajax: "Αξίζει κάτι;" Ntemis: "Αξίζει ό,τι ο Βισβίκης ήθελε να δείξει μ' αυτό. Τώρα πρέπει... να του το πάμε."
6. Clio: "Σας το είπα." Ajax: "...Στο νεκροταφείο." Clio: "Σας. Το. Είπα."

**Inventory**: + Πράσινη Πέτρα (Jade)

**Gameplay**: Enter cave (light → dark transition). Examine wall markings, find "Λ.Β. 1887." Search wrong spots first. Clio finds stone (she looks up). Green stone goes to inventory.

**Music**: Underground wonder. Echoing, dripping, mysterious but warm — not scary. Resonant crystalline tone when stone is found. The jade has its own sound.

---

### SCENE 9 — ΤΟ ΝΕΚΡΟΤΑΦΕΙΟ (The Captain's Rest)

*Dusk. The old cemetery above town. Marble headstones with carved ships. Cypress trees. Wild thyme.*

**Story beats**:
1. Every headstone is a biography — ships carved in marble. Clio reads them aloud.
2. They find Visvikis's grave: marble captain's headstone, carved schooner, 1841-1903.
3. Ntemis places the green stone on the headstone. Silence.
4. The air changes. Not cold — different. Clio grabs Ntemis's hand. Ajax steps back.
5. **The ghost of Captain Visvikis appears.** Not frightening — weathered, proud, tired. Captain's coat, white beard. He smiles: the smile of a man who has waited 120 years.
6. He doesn't speak. He points down the hill, toward the harbor, toward **Agios Nikolaos church**. Traces a cross in the air — but also a compass rose.
7. Clio whispers: "Ο χάρτης. Θέλει να βρούμε τον χάρτη. Είναι στον Άγιο Νικόλαο." The ghost nods. Fades. The green stone glows once, then goes dark.
8. Ajax: "...Μόλις μας έδωσε οδηγίες ένα φάντασμα." Ntemis: "Μόλις μας έδωσε οδηγίες ο προπάππους μου."
9. Clio draws the ghost in her notebook: "Κουρασμένος αλλά χαρούμενος."

**Gameplay**: Explore graveyard — read 3-4 captain headstones. Find Visvikis's grave. Use green stone on headstone → ghost sequence. Ghost points to church → fades. Clio interprets.

**Music**: THE GHOST'S THEME. Solo Greek lyra. Lonely and melancholy → warms as the ghost smiles → quiet as he fades → resolves into peace.

---

### SCENE 10 — ΑΓΙΟΣ ΝΙΚΟΛΑΟΣ (The Map in the Church)

*Church interior at candlelight. Icons gleaming gold. The famous wooden iconostasis.*

**Story beats**:
1. Ntemis: "Πάτερ, ψάχνουμε κάτι που άφησε ο Καπετάν Βισβίκης." Papas: Visvikis was the church's greatest benefactor — paid for the roof, the icons, the iconostasis.
2. They search. Ntemis examines icons. Ajax tries to climb the iconostasis. Papas: "ΟΧΙ στις εικόνες!"
3. Everyone looks up. Clio sits quietly, looking at the floor.
4. Clio: "Μπαμπά. Το πάτωμα." A floor tile near the altar has Visvikis's mark — the same "Λ.Β." from the cave. It's loose.
5. Underneath: **a rolled nautical chart**, sealed in oilcloth. Protected for over a century. Hand-drawn. The Corinthian Gulf, Galaxidi, and marked with an X: the tiny island of **Άγιος Γεώργιος**.
6. Margin note in Visvikis's hand: "Το βράχι κοιτάει νοτιοδυτικά. Κάτω από τον σταυρό, πέντε βήματα. Σκάψε."
7. Ajax: "Ξέρω αυτό το νησάκι! Μπορούμε να πάμε με βάρκα!" Papas: "Αν βρείτε κάτι... θυμηθείτε ποιος το σώζει. Αυτή η πόλη χρειάζεται θαύματα."

**Inventory**: + Ναυτικός Χάρτης

**Gameplay**: Talk to Papas. Examine icons, iconostasis (wrong spots). Ajax climbing (comic beat). Clio spots floor tile with "Λ.Β." Open tile → chart. Map to inventory → exit to harbor/boat.

**Music**: Sacred warmth. Deep choral tones with warm reverb. Swells when map discovered — adventure theme returns, now richer.

---

### SCENE 11 — ΤΟ ΚΑΡΑΒΙ (The Crossing)

*Dawn. The boat Ελπίδα on the gulf. Galaxidi receding behind them. Agios Georgios ahead.*

**Story beats**:
1. Chrysostomos: "Ο πατέρας σου μου είπε ότι κάποια μέρα θα ερχόσουν. Δεν πίστευα ότι θα ζούσα να το δω."
2. The crossing is short but magical. Galaxidi seen from the water — the town in its landscape, mountains behind, the gulf stretching.
3. Chrysostomos tells Visvikis's story: "Ήταν ο τελευταίος ρομαντικός. Ταξίδεψε μέχρι την Κίνα κι επέστρεψε. Έλεγε ότι βρήκε κάτι που θα σώσει το Γαλαξίδι. Κανείς δεν τον πίστεψε. Πέθανε μόνος. Αλλά δεν πέθανε χωρίς σχέδιο."
4. They land on the rocky islet. A tiny chapel. Wild thyme and oregano. Seagulls wheeling.
5. The rock that faces southwest. The old stone cross. Five steps.
6. Ajax digs. Clio counts the steps. Ntemis watches. The shovel hits wood.

**Gameplay**: Board boat (scenic transition). Click landmarks during crossing for stories. Arrive at island. Find southwest rock → cross → count 5 steps. Dig → transition to treasure.

**Music**: THE MOST BEAUTIFUL MUSIC IN THE GAME. Acoustic guitar and lyra. Dawn on the water. A melody that aches with hope.

---

### SCENE 12 — Ο ΘΗΣΑΥΡΟΣ (The Treasure)

*Close-up: an old sea chest, iron-bound, emerging from the earth. Morning light. Galaxidi across the water.*

**Story beats**:
1. A sea chest. Old, iron-bound. Visvikis built things to last. They open it.
2. Inside: **Gold coins** (Ottoman, Venetian, ancient Greek). **A jade necklace** matching the green stone. **The original αλληλασφάλεια ledger** — the mutual insurance pact of 1860 with 104 captain signatures. The real one. And **a letter**:

> *Αυτός ο θησαυρός δεν είναι δικός μου. Είναι του Γαλαξειδίου.*
>
> *Ταξίδεψα στην Ανατολή και γύρισα πλούσιος. Αλλά ο αληθινός πλούτος δεν είναι ο χρυσός. Είναι αυτό που χτίσαμε μαζί — η αλληλεγγύη. Κανείς δεν ταξιδεύει μόνος.*
>
> *Το Γαλαξίδι σβήνει. Τα καράβια φεύγουν. Ο κόσμος αλλάζει κι εμείς δεν αλλάζουμε μαζί του. Αλλά αυτός ο θησαυρός μπορεί να ξαναχτίσει αυτό που χάσαμε — αν αυτός που τον βρει αγαπάει αυτή την πόλη αρκετά.*
>
> ***Κράτα την αλληλεγγύη. Άλλαξε το πλοίο.***
>
> *— Καπ. Λ. Βισβίκης*

3. Silence. Waves and gulls.
4. Clio: "Μπαμπά, τι θα κάνουμε;" Ntemis: "Αυτό που ήθελε ο Βισβίκης. Θα τα δώσουμε στο Γαλαξίδι."
5. Ajax reads the old signatures: "Εκατό τέσσερις. Εκατό τέσσερις άνθρωποι υπέγραψαν. Κανένας δεν ταξιδεύει μόνος."
6. Clio: "Αν ο Βισβίκης μπορούσε να δει τώρα, θα χαμογελούσε;" Ntemis: "Ναι, μικρή μου. Νομίζω ότι τώρα, μπορεί επιτέλους να κοιμηθεί."

**Music**: Building from silence. Each item revealed adds an instrument — coins (percussion), necklace (lyra), ledger (strings), letter (full theme). When Clio speaks: a new melody. Gentle. Hopeful. The sound of the future.

### EPILOGUE

*Εκατόν είκοσι χρόνια αργότερα, τρεις ταξιδιώτες τον βρήκαν.*

*Κρατήστε το σύμφωνο. Αλλάξτε το πλοίο.*

## Dialog Guidelines

### Tone
- Warm, never sarcastic. These characters love each other.
- Mystery is gentle, not threatening. Wonder, not danger.
- Greek dialog should sound natural — everyday family speech, not literary.
- Humor comes from character (Clio's deadpan logic, Ajax's enthusiasm, Ntemis's quiet reactions).

### Per-Character Voice

| Character | Style | Example |
|-----------|-------|---------|
| **Ntemis** | Calm, reflective, gentle | "Ας κοιτάξουμε πιο προσεκτικά." |
| **Ajax** | Excited, questions, action | "Τέλειο! Πάμε αμέσως!" |
| **Clio** | Observant, deadpan, wise | "Σας. Το. Είπα." |
| **Narrator** | Descriptive, atmospheric | "Ο ήλιος βάφει χρυσά τα καλντερίμια." |
| **Stavros** | Weathered, knowing, careful | "Πρόσεξε. Ο Βισβίκης δεν ήταν απλός καπετάνιος." |
| **Curator** | Passionate, educational | "Ακριβώς, μικρέ μου. Ακριβώς αυτό." |
| **Athos** | Sharp, precise, lawyerly warmth | "Μη με ρωτήσεις αν αξίζεις. Πήγαινε και δείξ' το." |
| **Akis** | Warm, knowing, musical | "Αν ψάχνεις για τον Βισβίκη, πρέπει να δεις το μουσείο." |
| **Stathis** | Easy-going, poetic, nostalgic | "Ήταν το τραγούδι των ναυτικών. Το τραγουδούσαν όταν έφευγαν." |
| **Giannis** | Quiet, observant, few words | "Ξέρεις γιατί αγόρασα τον μύλο; Γιατί από εδώ βλέπεις τα πάντα." |
| **Papas** | Warm, mischievous | "ΟΧΙ στις εικόνες!" |
| **Chrysostomos** | Old sailor, poetic | "Δεν πίστευα ότι θα ζούσα να το δω." |

### Dialog Rules
- Max 2 sentences per speech bubble
- Narrator describes, characters react
- Children should sound like children (but smart ones)
- Important revelations get their own dialog beat (pause before/after)

## Puzzle Design Principles

1. **Logical** — every puzzle makes sense in context
2. **Character-driven** — use each family member's strengths (Ajax reaches/digs, Clio observes/reads, Ntemis knows/connects)
3. **Progressive** — teach mechanics early, combine later
4. **No dead ends** — player can always progress (no pixel hunting)
5. **Rewarding** — puzzles reveal story, not just unlock doors

## Historical Foundation

- 300 ships at peak (1870), 6,000 residents
- αλληλασφάλεια founded 1860, 104 signers, Agia Paraskevi church
- Decline: refusal to adopt steam power
- Last sailing ship "Chrysoula" launched 1904
- Karkaros cave: real cave behind Plateia Mama, tunnel carved 1940
- Agios Georgios: real islet offshore
- Agios Nikolaos: real church in Galaxidi
